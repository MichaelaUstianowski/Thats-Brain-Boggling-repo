The Temporal Lobe

The Temporal Lobe is home to the Primary Auditory Cortex, where auditory information is first processed. The Temporal Lobe is also involved with understanding language and is linked with object recognition (see the Occipital Lobe page for more information).

The Primary Auditory Cortex

The Primary Auditory cortex is an area in the Temporal Lobe. It is the first area for auditory information from the ear is processed and is sent to different areas in order to be processed further. For example, if you hear a word, the Primary Auditory cortex will recieve the information first, then activate other areas to provide more detail such as word meaning.

Wernicke's Area

This is an area in the Temporal lobe and is close to the parietal lobe. This area is involved with speech comprehension as people who have dammage to this area are known to have a disorder called Wernicke's Aphasia, where individuals struggle to understand speech.
