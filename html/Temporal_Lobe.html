The Temporal Lobe

The Temporal Lobe is home to the Pirmary Auditory Cortex, where auditory information is first processed. The Temporal Lobe is also involved with understanding language and is linked with object recognition (see the Occipital Lobe page for more information).

The Primary Auditory Cortex

The Primary Auditory cortex is an area in the Temporal Lobe. It is the first area for auditory information to be processed and to be sent to different areas in order to be processed further. For example if you hear a word, the Primary Auditory cortex will recieve the information first, then to recognise the word meaning another area will be active to provide extra detail on what we heard.

Wernicke's Area

This is an area in the Temporal lobe and is close to the parietal lobe. This area is involved with speech comprehension as people who have dammage to this area are known to have a disorder called Wernicke's Aphasia, where individuals struggle to understand speech.
